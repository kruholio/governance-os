## 2. The System Stack (Layer Model)

### L0: Quantitative Metrology (Physical Grounding)

The foundational layer **L0** establishes the physical interface between the material world and the digital logic of the kernel. It ensures that the data fed into **L1** and **L2** is not just abundant, but mathematically and scientifically valid. 

#### Resolution vs. Accuracy
In a data-driven governance system, we distinguish between the granularity of observation and the truth of the representation:

- **Resolution ($Res$):** The smallest detectable change in a social or ecological variable. This defines the sensitivity of the feedback loops in **L2 (Voting Core)**. High resolution without accuracy results in "precise noise".

- **Accuracy ($Acc$):** The conformity of captured data to physical reality. According to the axiom **"Physics wins,"** political goals must be measured against calibrated natural constants. Systemic bias (low accuracy) is a direct violation of the **"Love is love"** constraint if it leads to distorted representation.


#### Data Acquisition: Sampling & Information Loss
The transformation of continuous societal processes into discrete "Pull Requests" follows the laws of information theory:

- **Sampling Rate ($f_s$):** To avoid **Aliasing**, the frequency of data collection (e.g., resource consumption, demographic shifts) must satisfy $f_s > 2 \cdot f_{max}$. Insufficient sampling rates lead to delayed governance responses, where trends are only recognized after they reach destructive amplitudes (entropy increase).

- **Quantization Error:** Every measurement reduces complex reality to discrete values. The **Albert Einstein Loss** is often a failure of L0 resolution: if the system cannot "measure" a specific talent or potential, that potential remains invisible to the OS and is lost to humanity.


#### Metrological Constraints
- **Observability Requirement:** Any system state that cannot be measured with an accuracy of $\epsilon < 3\%$ is considered "unstable" and cannot trigger automated L2 corrections.

- **Noise Filtering:** Political arbitrariness is treated as statistical noise. The **ISAC (L3)** acts as  a low-pass filter to ensure only signal (evidence-based data) reaches the application layer.

### Libraries, sources, and potential data quality limitations must be defined and named at the inception of a branch; otherwise, the system will not maintain a stable state.

### L2: Hardware Abstraction (VC)

The **Voting Core (VC)** acts as a statistical “digital twin” of society.

- **Algorithm:** Uses the Hamilton method to map the population with demographic accuracy.

- **Self-healing:** Automatic correction processes for deviations in representation of more than ±3%.

---
### L3: The runtime environment (governance processes)

Politics is carried out here as a **scientific experiment protocol**:

- **Git principle:** Every measure is a pull request (PR). Every change is versioned (commits, rollbacks).

- **Linter/compiler (ISAC):** The Independent Scientific Audit Council checks PRs for physical feasibility and logical errors before they are merged.

---
### L4: Application Layer (Policies)

This is where the actual programs run (e.g., education reforms, climate protection policies). This layer is highly agile and is constantly validated by real-world data.
