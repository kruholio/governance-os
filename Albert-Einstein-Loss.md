**Albert-Einstein-Loss:** The Thermodynamic Potential Proof treats human dignity as a physical constant ($P_H \approx 20\text{W}$), defining systemic bias (racism, poverty, etc.) as energetic waste (entropy) that mathematically reduces our collective cognitive efficiency. Any law or system that increases this loss ($L_{AE}$) is a thermodynamic failure—true stability only occurs when every "20-Watt unit" is optimized.

### 1. The Biological Constant ($P_H$)

We define the **Human Entropy Barrier** as the basal energetic load of the central nervous system.

$$P_H \approx 20\text{ W}$$

This constant serves as the physical equivalent of human dignity. It remains independent of cognitive output variables, as it characterizes the baseline existential capacity of the processor (the brain).

### 2. The Loss Equation ($L_{AE}$)

The **Albert Einstein Loss** describes the delta between systemically available total power and the cognitive work actually harnessed for the species.

$$L_{AE} = \sum_{i=1}^{N} \int_{t_0}^{t_1} \left( P_H \cdot (1 - \sigma_i(t)) \right) dt \rightarrow \min$$

- $N$: Total population.
    
- $\sigma_i(t)$: Efficiency coefficient of individual $i$ at time $t$.
    
- $\sigma \in [0, 1]$: A value driven toward $1$ by systemic barriers (poverty, discrimination, caloric deficit).
    

### 3. Thermodynamic Optimization (Axiom 3)

Any form of **bias** (racism, sexism, classism) functions physically as an **insulator** or **resistor** within the network.

- **Heat Dissipation:** Energy expended to maintain hierarchies is entropy—lost to the resolution of global primary problems ($L_0$).
    
- **The Proof:** A system reaches its energetic minimum (maximum stability) exclusively when $\sigma_i$ is maximized for all $i$. Since $P_H$ is constant, any inequity constitutes energetic waste.
